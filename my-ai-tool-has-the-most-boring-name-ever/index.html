My AI Tool Has the Most Boring Name Ever

It generates topic clusters, it ships, and the name is still mid. Some things matter more than others.

If you've ever tried to come up with content ideas and ended up doomscrolling instead, same. Staring at a blank doc, trying to plan blog posts, video scripts, or landing pages, wondering what people actually want to read. It's a specific kind of paralysis.

So I built something to fix it.

Using ChatGPT to Do One Thing Well

I created a custom tool powered by ChatGPT to take the stress and guesswork out of content planning. No tab juggling. No trend chasing. No scrolling through keyword lists that go nowhere.

Type in a keyword or a question. It returns a clean, structured set of related content ideas grouped by topic. The AI handles the connections. Your brain takes over from there.

When I typed in homes for sale in Austin I got:

  • Neighborhood-specific ideas like "South Congress homes" and "Zilker listings"
  • Buyer-focused angles like "first-time buyer tips" and "best time to buy in Austin"
  • Market context like "Austin housing trends" and "property tax breakdown"

Not a flat list. Ideas that are already connected — the way a real content strategy actually works.

Who It's For

I built this for marketers, car dealerships, bloggers, real estate pros, overwhelmed freelancers, social media managers, and anyone else who needs to create content and doesn't want to lose an hour figuring out where to start.

Fast. Structured. No second-guessing. Whether you're building a strategy from scratch or filling in the gaps, it meets you where you are.

The Name

Still mid. Probably always will be. But the Topic Cluster Tool (FKA "Keyword Tool") works, and that's the part that matters.
